\noindent

Lung cancer remains one of the leading causes of cancer-related mortality worldwide, largely due to late-stage detection and reliance on specialist radiologists for CT scan interpretation. LungInsight addresses these challenges by developing a multimodal, explainable AI framework that integrates CT imaging and structured clinical data to enable early and accurate lung cancer prediction. The system employs a DenseNet-based CT image encoder and an MLP-based clinical feature encoder, with late fusion combining both learned representations to jointly predict malignancy probability and disease stage. Training is performed on the LIDC-IDRI dataset paired with National Lung Screening Trial clinical records, using AdamW optimisation with focal loss to handle class imbalance effectively.

Explainability is central to the design: Grad-CAM generates localised heatmaps highlighting suspicious nodule regions in CT slices, while SHAP values quantify the contribution of individual clinical variables such as smoking history, age, and lung function to each prediction. The framework is evaluated using accuracy, ROC-AUC, F1-score, PR-AUC, Brier score, and calibration curves, with ablation studies comparing imaging-only, clinical-only, and fused configurations. A Streamlit-based clinical decision support dashboard enables clinicians to upload CT scans and patient records, run real-time inference, and review explainable risk assessments — making LungInsight a trustworthy, interpretable tool for supporting early lung cancer diagnosis and confident clinical decision-making.
